Reading

This is a general overview of the levels of reading, with parts to our first topic we will cover, Pre-Reading

  1. Pre-Reading:
    • Eye Clarity
    • Eye Focus
    • Eye Tracking
  2. Inspectional Reading
  3. Analytical Reading
  4. Syntoptical Reading

Reading is a complex task, it requires a lot of active participation on behalf of the reader. Below you will find a table that lists some important levels of reading around their respective tasks you must accomplish in them. Note that this is not an exhaustive list but a general idea of each level of reading. Each level has something unique or interesting about it. However, the further levels of reading are built upon the backbone of the earlier levels. Which makes the Pre-Reading level of reading the most important level to reading as any reading cannot be done without this level being sufficiently met.


For the first level of reading, Pre-Reading, we are mainly concerned with the abilities required to read words in the first place. These abilities are things such as eye clarity, which is the ability for the eyes to see something clearly, eye focus, which is the ability for the eye to look at an object and focus their vision on it, and eye tracking, which is the ability for one to sufficiently follow the words on the page at a proper pace that allows them to read successfully.


Below you will see a table that is a general overview of the levels and a few components of each of them.

The 4 Levels of Reading
Pre-Reading Inspectional Reading Analytical Reading Syntoptical Reading
Element 1 Eye Clarity Inspect Table of Contents Analyze the Author's Message Formulate a question that hasn't been asked
Element 2 Eye Focus Determine Author's Goal Analyze how the Author attempts to prove their message Read material in close proximity to your question
Element 3 Eye Tracking Divide Author's Message into constituent parts Agree or Disagree with Author's message; support reasons why or why not Bring together the different readings and synthesize new understandings to solve your question

The more difficult words are when trying to read them the more mental power is wasted on converting the words into something intelligible and less on understanding what we're reading.
